Keyboards, keyboards, keyboards. The one thing people with a computer use most, yet almost always cheap out on.

<!--more-->

Having a tool that fits your use-case perfectly feels like heaven. Now imagine that, but on a keyboard; the thing *you* use daily to interact with computers, configured exactly how you want it. A perfected layout with your most used functions available at a glance, precisely-tuned feedback, superb portability and just the right number of keys to perfectly fit your hand. That is a typist's dream. 

Simply put, a nice keyboard will make you happy when you work with it. As silly as this sounds, it is true: having good tools will not necessarily make you much better, faster or more efficient at your job, but it definitely will put a smile on your face while you use it—and that smile *will* make you better at whatever you're doing.
