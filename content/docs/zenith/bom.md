## Structure
Depending on your configuration, you will need a number of printed parts in order to build the outer structure of your filter.

{{< details title="Base" closed="true" >}}

Zenith Base is the original configuration designed to filter particulates. This version features a two-filter one-fan assembly and requires the least number of parts.

- [(x4) Base Wall](https://github.com/typewatch/zenith/tree/main/3dfiles)
- [(x2) Base Grill](https://github.com/typewatch/zenith/tree/main/3dfiles)
- [(x1) Mount](https://github.com/typewatch/zenith/tree/main/3dfiles)

{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Combo" closed="true" >}}

Zenith Combo is equipped to filter particulates and VOCs. This version is slightly bigger than the original to house the necessary carbon filter media and refilling mechanism.

## Filters
You can use pretty much any filter you want and cut it to size. I recommend you steer clear of higher MERV ratings because while they do their job perfectly fine, they impair airflow and thus lower your overall filtering capability.

I used MERV10 filters I got from Home Depot and they seem to work fine.

## Filter Media
Try to find non-acidic activated charcoal. The acidic stuff will rust the inside of your printer in a pinch.
